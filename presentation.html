Why Radar Activity Classification?

Traditional Challenges

  • 📷 Computer vision requires line-of-sight and lighting
  • 🔒 Privacy concerns with cameras
  • ❌ Limited performance in occluded/dark conditions

Our Solution: Radar Micro-Doppler Signals

  • ✅ Works through walls and in darkness
  • ✅ Privacy-preserving (no visual data)
  • ✅ Captures detailed motion signatures

6 Activities Classified

🚶 Walking 🪑 Sitting down ⬆️ Standing up
🎁 Pick up object 💧 Drink water ⚠️ Fall

From Raw Radar to Machine Learning

Three Complementary Representations

Channel Representation Captures
1 Spectrogram Frequency content over time
2 Range-Time Object distance vs. time
3 Range-Doppler Distance vs. velocity signatures

All normalized to 64×64 → [3, 64, 64] tensor per sample

Three-Stream CNN Architecture

Input [B, 3, 64, 64]
    ↓
    ├─→ Stream 1 (Spectrogram) ────→ 4-Block CNN → 256-d
    ├─→ Stream 2 (Range-Time) ─────→ 4-Block CNN → 256-d
    └─→ Stream 3 (Range-Doppler) ──→ 4-Block CNN → 256-d
    
                  Concatenation
                       ↓
              768-d fused vector
                       ↓
         Classifier (768 → 384 → 192 → 6)
                       ↓
            Output: Class probabilities

Key: Late fusion allows each stream to learn specialized representations
